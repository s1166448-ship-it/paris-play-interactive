The Judgment of Paris Plate
Qing Dynasty, Qianlong Reign (c. 1745) | Famille Rose Porcelain | Diameter: 22.8 cm
This famille rose porcelain plate is a classic example of 18th-century Chinese export porcelain made for the European market. Its central design depicts the Greek myth of the Judgment of Paris: the Trojan prince Paris awards the golden apple to Venus, the goddess of love, among Juno, Athena, and Venus. Painted entirely in rich magenta (carmine) with gilt details, the plate features a Delft-style border popular in the 1740s–1750s. This piece exemplifies the fusion of Chinese craftsmanship, Western mythology, and European aesthetic taste.
